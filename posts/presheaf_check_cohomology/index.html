Cech Cohomology of Pre-Sheaves

Oct 05, 2018
2 min read

Let \(X\) be a topological space and let \(F\) be a pre-sheaf of vector spaces on \(X\). Given an open cover \(\mathcal{U} = \{ U_i \}_{I \in \Lambda}\) of \(X\), we define the Cech Cohomology of \(F\) as the cohomology of the cochain complex

\[ (C^\bullet (\mathcal{U},F), \delta^\bullet) \hspace{0.5cm} {\rm where} \hspace{0.5cm} C^p (\mathcal{U},F) = \prod\limits_{|I| = p + 1} F(U_I) \hspace{0.5cm} {\rm for}\hspace{0.5cm} I \in N(\mathcal{U}). \]

where \(U_I = \cap_{i \in I} U_i\). In order to define the differential \(\delta\), we need to choose an ordering of the indexing set \(\Lambda\). Thus, given a cochain \(s \in C^p (\mathcal{U},F)\), its image is defined on each coordinate as

\[ (\delta^p (s))_I = \sum_{k = 0}^{p+1} (-1)^k \rho_{U_I, U_{I(k)}}(s_{I(k)}) \]

where \(I = \{i_0,\ldots, i_{p+1}\}\subset \Lambda\), \(i_0 < i_2 < \ldots < i_{p+1}\) the ordering inherited from \(\Lambda\) and \(I(k)\) denotes \(I \setminus \{i_k\}\). Therefore the \(p^{\rm th}\) Cech Cohomology group of \(F\) is defined by the quotient

\[ {\check H}^p(\mathcal{U}, F) = \frac{{\rm ker}(\delta^p)}{{\rm im}(\delta^{p-1})} \hspace{0.5cm} {\rm i.e.} \hspace{0.5cm} H^p(C^\bullet (\mathcal{U},F)). \]

In order to ensure that the above definition is correct, we need to prove the following lemma.

The differential \(\delta\) in the cochain complex \(C^\bullet (\mathcal{U},F)\) for a cover \(\mathcal{U}\) and a pre-sheaf \(F\), satisfies the identity \(\delta^{p+1} \circ \delta^p = 0\) for all \(p \in \mathbb{Z}\).

Let \(s \in C^p (\mathcal{U},F)\), we focus on each coordinate \(I \subset \Lambda\) with \(|I| = p + 3\)

\( \begin{align*} (\delta^{p+1} \circ \delta^p (s))_I &= \sum_{k = 0}^{p+2} (-1)^k \rho_{U_I, U_{I(k)}}(\delta^p(s)_{I(k)}) \\ &=\sum_{k = 0}^{p+2}(-1)^k \left( \sum_{j = 0}^{k-1} (-1)^j \rho_{U_I, U_{I(k)}} \rho_{U_{I(k)}, U_{I(j,k)}}(s_{I(j,k)}) + \sum_{j=k+1}^{p+2} (-1)^{j-1} \rho_{U_I, U_{I(k)}} \rho_{U_{I(k)}, U_{I(k,j)}}(s_{I(k, j)}) \right) \\ &=\sum_{k = 0}^{p+2}\sum_{j = 0}^{k-1} (-1)^{k + j} \rho_{U_I, U_{I(k)}} \rho_{U_{I(k)}, U_{I(j,k)}}(s_{I(j,k)}) + \sum_{k = 0}^{p+2} \sum_{j=k+1}^{p+2}(-1)^{j + (k -1)} \rho_{U_I, U_{I(j)}} \rho_{U_{I(j)}, U_{I(j,k)}}(s_{I(j,k)}) \\ &=\sum \limits_{k = 0}^{p+2} \sum \limits_{j = 0}^{k-1} \left( (-1)^{k + j} \rho_{U_I, U_{I(k)}} \rho_{U_{I(k)}, U_{I(j,k)}}(s_{I(j,k)}) + (-1)^{j + (k -1)} \rho_{U_I, U_{I(j)}} \rho_{U_{I(j)}, U_{I(j,k)}}(s_{I(j,k)}) \right) = 0 \end{align*} \) where the last equality follow from the correspondence between the pairs \((k,j)\) in the first sum with the pairs \((k',j')=(j,k)\) from the second sum, and the commutativity of the following two diagrams:

$$ \xymatrix{ \ & U_I^{(j,k)} & \\ U_I^{(j)} \ar[ur] & \ & U_I^{(k)} \ar[ul]\\ \ & U_I \ar[ur] \ar[ul] \ar[uu] } $$
